What is Email Verification, and Why Is It Important?

Email verification is the process of confirming whether an email address is active. The goal is to ensure your email list is healthy, has a good sender reputation, and has low bounce rates.

When your emails bounce too often, it can hurt your email reputation and sender score because mail service providers won’t be able to deliver those to those invalid email addresses. You will still be paying for the attempt, though.

By validating emails, you won’t waste your time and money on junk leads.

It’s common for email addresses to become inactive over time. People might abandon email addresses they no longer use or they might leave their company and their corporate emails become inactive.

Every business must use an email verification tool to avoid potential negative consequences. And if you plan on growing your email list, then it’ll ensure your messages aren’t getting sent straight to spam. Here’s why you need it:

  • Reduce Bounce Rates: When you send emails to invalid or non-existent email addresses, they bounce back. This can harm your sender’s reputation and email deliverability. Email verification tools help you identify and remove such addresses, reducing bounce rates.
  • Improve Deliverability: A clean email list with verified addresses improves your chances of your emails reaching recipients’ inboxes instead of being marked as spam.
  • Save Costs: Sending emails to invalid addresses wastes money since most email service providers charge based on the number of contacts on your list.
  • Protect Your Reputation: Consistently sending emails to invalid addresses can negatively affect your sender’s reputation. A poor sender reputation can result in your emails being filtered as spam or not delivered at all.

And, if you’re sending emails through your WordPress site, you’ll also need to properly configure your WordPress email settings to ensure your customers are receiving your messages.

How Does Email Verification Tools Work?

An email verification tool is software designed to clean up your email list by removing inactive and invalid email addresses. They help to enhance email deliverability and the effectiveness of your email campaigns.

The verification process involves several steps:

  1. Syntax Check: The software examines email addresses for correct formatting, including the user name, “@” symbol, domain name, and top-level domain such as .com or .net. Email addresses should follow specific character limits and permissible characters.
  2. Blacklist Check: In this step, the tool compares your email list against a database of known issues, such as bots, spam traps, and fraudulent emails.
  3. MX Records Check: The system checks the MX (Mail Exchange) records of the email domains to confirm their legitimacy. MX records specify the mail servers authorized to accept messages for a domain.
  4. SMTP Protocol: The tool uses the SMTP (Simple Mail Transfer Protocol) to ping the email mailboxes. Based on the server’s response, it determines whether the address is ‘OK,’ ‘Doesn’t Exist,’ or ‘Disabled.’

You can verify individual contact information by entering a single email in the search box, or you can verify your entire database through bulk email verification.

The best email verification tools seamlessly integrate with your CRM, email marketing software, marketing automation platforms, and landing page builders. This integration allows you to easily transfer and clean your data from the tools you already use.
