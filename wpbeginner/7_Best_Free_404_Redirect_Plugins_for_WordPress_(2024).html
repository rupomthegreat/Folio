Why Is Your WordPress Site Showing a 404 Error?

Before you start fixing a 404 error, it is important to understand why WordPress outputs this error message.

It is one of the common WordPress problems that beginners encounter. It happens when a visitor goes to visit a post or page on your website that doesn‚Äôt exist at the address they entered.

A 404 error can happen if you change a website page‚Äôs URL structure, a .htaccess file gets corrupted, or your site switches to a new domain.

Another reason for the error can be deleting a WordPress post or page. Doing that will output the 404 or not found error.

Similarly, changing the URL of a blog post and not updating the internal links to the new location could lead to this problem.
